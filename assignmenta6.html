  • Step 2: Adjusting STL file with Rhino and 3D printing

    • For this assignment I used a model from Thingiverse as my base. I used Rhino to adjust the model (added sprues and keys) and converted mesh to solid. I split the new model in half for my mold.

    I failed a couple times because my 'Part 1 - Mold's Mold warped. Thus, I added a base to the gcode tp prevent the edges from warping. I also used tape to make sure the base wouldn't come off.

  • Step 3: Oomoo & Plaster

    • Oommo was easier to use. I used a 1:1 ratio and eyed it in a cup. After pouring it in the 3D mold, I left it to cure for 6 hours. I repeated this process twice to have four complete parts. After 'Part 2' dried, I then proceeded to use Plaster at 3:1 ratio to complete 'Part 3' the duck. This took two days to cure but I was inpatient and opened one but the head did not fully cure to the body.

Machine Settings: 3D printing. Dremel set to 325 degrees for PLA and Bed Tempature of 40 degrees.

Kit Design and Execution

  • Design Description: This week I did not design my model (duck). However, I did have to design the mold of the model in Rhino.
  • Execution: I used Rhino to save my design file as an STL. Then used Dremel to figure out the gcode to 3D print. After 3D printing my mold's mold I used Oomoo to create the mold. After Oomoo had cured, I used plaster to cast the mold of the duck.
